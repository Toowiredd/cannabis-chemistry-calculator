# Academic References — Cannabis Chemistry Calculator

This document lists the peer-reviewed academic citations used inline throughout the Knowledge tab.

## Required Key Papers

### 1. Filer 2022 — Acidic Cannabinoid Decarboxylation
- **Citation:** Filer, C. N. (2022). Acidic cannabinoid decarboxylation. *Cannabis and Cannabinoid Research*, 7(4), 467–477.
- **DOI:** [10.1089/can.2021.0072](https://doi.org/10.1089/can.2021.0072)
- **Cited for:** The 0.877 molecular weight ratio (THC/THCA = 314.45/358.47) used in the theoretical maximum THC calculation.

### 2. Wang et al. 2016 — Decarboxylation Kinetics
- **Citation:** Wang, M., Wang, Y. H., Avula, B., Radwan, M. M., Wanas, A. S., Mehmedic, Z., ... & ElSohly, M. A. (2016). Decarboxylation study of acidic cannabinoids: A novel approach using ultra-high-performance supercritical fluid chromatography/atmospheric pressure chemical ionization quadrupole-time of flight mass spectrometry. *Cannabis and Cannabinoid Research*, 1(1), 262–271.
- **DOI:** [10.1089/can.2016.0020](https://doi.org/10.1089/can.2016.0020)
- **Cited for:** THCA decarboxylation kinetics, first-order reaction behavior, temperature-dependent conversion rates.

### 3. Moreno et al. 2020 — Comparative CBDA/THCA Kinetics
- **Citation:** Moreno, T., Dyer, P., & Tallon, S. (2020). Cannabinoid decarboxylation: A comparative kinetic study. *Industrial & Engineering Chemistry Research*, 59(46), 20387–20393.
- **DOI:** [10.1021/acs.iecr.0c03791](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.iecr.0c03791)
- **Cited for:** Comparative decarboxylation kinetics of CBDA vs THCA, activation energy range (85–100 kJ/mol), Arrhenius modeling.

### 4. MacCallum & Russo 2018 — Clinical Dosing Framework
- **Citation:** MacCallum, C. A., & Russo, E. B. (2018). Practical considerations in medical cannabis administration and dosing. *European Journal of Internal Medicine*, 49, 12–19.
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.ejim.2018.01.004](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ejim.2018.01.004)
- **Cited for:** Medical cannabis dose classification framework, starting-low-and-titrating-slow principle.

### 5. Eyal et al. 2023 — Terpene Volatility
- **Citation:** Eyal, A. M., Raz, N., & Davidson, E. M. (2023). Vapor pressure, vaping, and corrections to misconceptions related to medical cannabis. *Cannabis and Cannabinoid Research*, 8(2), 245–259.
- **DOI:** [10.1089/can.2021.0173](https://doi.org/10.1089/can.2021.0173)
- **Cited for:** Terpene vapor pressure, evaporation behavior at low temperatures, boiling point data for major cannabis terpenes.

### 6. Raz et al. 2022 — Terpene Formulation
- **Citation:** Raz, N., Eyal, A. M., & Davidson, E. M. (2022). Optimal treatment with cannabis extracts formulations is gained via knowledge of their terpene content and via enrichment with specifically selected monoterpenes and monoterpenoids. *Molecules*, 27(20), 6920.
- **DOI:** [10.3390/molecules27206920](https://doi.org/10.3390/molecules27206920)
- **Cited for:** Terpene retention strategies, formulation optimization, terpene-cannabinoid synergy.

### 7. Jaidee et al. 2022 — THC Degradation Kinetics
- **Citation:** Jaidee, W., Siridechakorn, I., Nessopa, S., Wisuitiprot, V., Chaiwangrach, N., Ingkaninan, K., & Waranuch, N. (2022). Kinetics of CBD, Δ9-THC degradation and cannabinol formation in cannabis resin at various temperature and pH conditions. *Cannabis and Cannabinoid Research*, 7(4), 537–547.
- **DOI:** [10.1089/can.2021.0004](https://doi.org/10.1089/can.2021.0004)
- **Cited for:** THC degradation to CBN over time, stability kinetics of cannabinoids during storage and heating. Note: paper measured 50–80°C in dried resin and 40–70°C in pH-2 solution; values at 4/25/40°C are extrapolations (see Audit Table).

### 8. Ramella et al. 2020 — Lipid Extraction
- **Citation:** Ramella, M., Scherhag, J. D., Bovolin, P., Tagliapietra, S., Galli, R., & Bignami, G. (2020). Impact of lipid sources on quality traits of medical cannabis-based oil preparations. *Molecules*, 25(13), 2986.
- **DOI:** [10.3390/molecules25132986](https://doi.org/10.3390/molecules25132986)
- **Cited for:** Extraction efficiency differences between carrier fats (olive oil vs MCT), cannabinoid profile stability in lipid matrices.

### 9. Bhaskar et al. 2021 — Consensus Dosing
- **Citation:** Bhaskar, A., Bell, A., Boileau, I., Bollan, K., Burke, M., Coulombe, J., ... & Moulin, D. E. (2021). Consensus recommendations on dosing and administration of medical cannabis to treat chronic pain: Results of a modified Delphi process. *Journal of Cannabis Research*, 3(1), 24.
- **DOI:** [10.1186/s42238-021-00073-1](https://doi.org/10.1186/s42238-021-00073-1)
- **Cited for:** Expert consensus dosing recommendations for chronic pain, microdose-to-low initial dosing (2.5–10 mg THC).

### 10. Tahir et al. 2021 — Biosynthesis
- **Citation:** Tahir, M. N., Raz, S., Rondeau-Gagné, S., & Trant, J. F. (2021). The biosynthesis of the cannabinoids. *Journal of Cannabis Research*, 3(1), 7.
- **DOI:** [10.1186/s42238-021-00062-4](https://doi.org/10.1186/s42238-021-00062-4)
- **Cited for:** Cannabinoid biosynthetic pathway, CBGA as precursor to THCA/CBDA/CBCA, enzymatic conversion mechanisms.

### 11. Garcia-Valverde et al. 2022 — Thermal Cascade
- **Citation:** Garcia-Valverde, M. T., Sánchez-Carnerero Callado, C., Díaz-Liñán, M. C., Sánchez de Medina, V., Hidalgo-García, J., Nadal, X., Hanuš, L., & Ferreiro-Vera, C. (2022). Effect of temperature in the degradation of cannabinoids: From a brief residence in the gas chromatography inlet port to a longer period in thermal treatments. *Frontiers in Chemistry*, 10, 1038729.
- **DOI:** [10.3389/fchem.2022.1038729](https://doi.org/10.3389/fchem.2022.1038729)
- **Cited for:** Temperature-driven degradation cascade (THCA → THC → CBN), thermal stability under various heating conditions.

## Supporting References

### 12. Perrotin-Brunel et al. 2011 — Decarboxylation Kinetics and Molecular Modeling
- **Citation:** Perrotin-Brunel, H., Buijs, H., van Spronsen, J., van Roosmalen, M. J. E., Peters, C. J., Verpoorte, R., & Witkamp, G. J. (2011). Decarboxylation of Δ9-tetrahydrocannabinol: Kinetics and molecular modeling. *Journal of Molecular Structure*, 987(1–3), 67–73.
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.molstruc.2010.11.062](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.molstruc.2010.11.062)
- **Cited for:** Early kinetic modeling of THCA decarboxylation, activation energy estimates, reaction mechanism studies.

### 13. Romano & Hazekamp 2013 — Cannabis Oil Chemical Evaluation
- **Citation:** Romano, L. L., & Hazekamp, A. (2013). Cannabis oil: Chemical evaluation of an upcoming cannabis-based medicine. *Phytomedicine*, 20(12), 1079–1088.
- **DOI:** [10.1055/s-0032-1327893](https://doi.org/10.1055/s-0032-1327893)
- **Cited for:** Comparative analysis of cannabis oil preparation methods, cannabinoid and terpene content across extraction techniques.

### 14. Citti et al. 2018 — CBDA Decarboxylation Kinetics
- **Citation:** Citti, C., Pacchetti, B., Vandelli, M. A., Forni, F., & Cannazza, G. (2018). Analysis of cannabinoids in commercial hemp seed oil and decarboxylation kinetics studies of cannabidiolic acid (CBDA). *Journal of Pharmaceutical and Biomedical Analysis*, 149, 532–540.
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.jpba.2017.11.044](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpba.2017.11.044)
- **Cited for:** CBDA decarboxylation kinetics in hemp seed oil, comparative kinetics with THCA, isomer-specific behavior.

### 15. Trofin et al. 2012 — Long-Term Resin Stability (cited via Jaidee 2022 reference list)
- **Citation (as cited by Jaidee 2022, ref 4):** Trofin, I. G., Dabija, G., Vaieanu, D. U., & Aboul-Enein, H. Y. (2012). The influence of long-term storage conditions on the stability of cannabinoids derived from cannabis resin. *Revista de Chimie (Bucharest)*, 63(4), 422–427.
- **Cited for:** Long-term storage stability of cannabis resin at room temperature; one of the data sources underpinning low-T (25–40°C) rate constants in `degradation.ts`. ⚠ DOI not independently verified — secondary citation only.

### 16. Lindholst 2010 — Cannabis Resin Long-Term Storage (cited via Jaidee 2022 reference list)
- **Citation (as cited by Jaidee 2022, ref 34):** Lindholst, C. (2010). Long term stability of cannabis resin and cannabis extracts. *Australian Journal of Forensic Sciences*, 42(3), 181–190.
- **Cited for:** Long-term kinetic data on cannabinoid degradation under ambient storage conditions; corroborates the 4–25°C rate constants in `degradation.ts`. ⚠ DOI not independently verified — secondary citation only.

### Notes on drift findings

**Drift #1 (Jaidee 2022 at low T):** The `degradation.ts` block-comment says "Rate constants based on Jaidee 2022 data" but Jaidee 2022 only measured rate constants in 50–80°C (dried resin) and 40–70°C (pH 2 solution) ranges. The 4°C/25°C/40°C rate constants are extrapolations below the measured window. Values are kept (they are conservative — engine says slower degradation than papers report at low T) but the comment is being softened to acknowledge the extrapolation.

**Drift #2 (doneness Arrhenius constants):** `Ea1=92 kJ/mol` is at the upper edge of Wang 2016's 84.8–88 kJ/mol range for THCA-A. `Ea2=110 kJ/mol`, `A1=1.5e12`, and `A2=2.0e12` are internal Arrhenius fits with no direct source match — pre-exponential factors should be ~10⁸ (per Wang 2016) not 10¹². Kept as engineering default but flagged.

**Drift #3 (no citation for fat extraction efficiencies):** DESIGN.md says ghee/coconut/MCT values "based on peer-reviewed extraction studies" — those studies are Ramella 2020 (#8) and Romano & Hazekamp 2013 (#13) but neither gives the exact 0.85/0.82/0.92 values used. The values are reasonable engineering estimates anchored to the literature but the specific numbers are TODOs.
